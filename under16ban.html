Australia's Under 16 Social Media Ban is a Terrible Idea That Won't Work

The government's age verification plan is a poorly thought-out surveillance scheme that invades everyone's privacy, while doing almost nothing to actually protect the youth.

Privacy Nightmare for Ordinary Users

This age verification system creates serious problems for regular users:

  • Massive Data Collection: It's outrageous that we'd be forced to hand over sensitive documents to private companies that can't even keep their own systems secure.
  • Loss of Anonymous Browsing: They're trying to eliminate online anonymity, putting whistleblowers, journalists, and vulnerable people at risk.
  • Mission Creep is Guaranteed: We all know this system won't stop at age verification, it's the start for broader monitoring and control.

A Dangerous Step Toward Surveillance

This approach pushes us toward a monitored dystopian internet:

  • Creating Digital Barriers: Why do we need to show ID just to use the internet? This is disturbing on so many levels.
  • Ineffective and Intrusive: The methods they're proposing either don't work properly or create dangerous security issues.
